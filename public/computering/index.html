Computering

Welcome to Computering! Home to all websites that be computering. (lit. "computer webring" but also "to computer")

This webring is a little club for websites and webmasters that are interested in, discuss, or otherwise style their pages after older computers and operating systems.

What's a webring?

According to everyone's favorite academic source;

A webring (or web ring) is a collection of websites linked together in a circular structure, usually organized around a specific theme, and often educational or social. They were popular in the 1990s and early 2000s, particularly among amateur websites.

Frequently asked questions

Q: Why is there no "official" CSS for the widget like other webrings have?

A: Because one of the main themes to this webring is sites that imitate specific OSes. It'd look very out of place if, say, I made the widget Windows 98 themed and someone with a MacOS themed site had to include it. Folks are encouraged to present the widget however they like and in whichever way suits their site best.

Rules

The rules to join computering are pretty liberal compared to others. I'd like it for everyone who is interested to be able to join regardless of their web host preference or other things like that. That being said...

  • Your website must not be a blank page
  • Your website must not feature any form of bigoted content (racism, misogyny, LGBTPhobia, etc)
  • Your website must not feature not safe for work or any form of explicit sexual content
  • Your website must be hand-made and hosted on a real web host, so no carrds, strawpages or alike
  • Your website must feature the computering widget, and have it on an easily accessible page
  • Your website may be hosted on any platform of your choice
  • You may customize the widget to your liking based on the boilerplate stylesheet
  • I reserve the right to remove your site from the webring or not approve your entrance into it if any of the above rules are broken
